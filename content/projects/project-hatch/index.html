[Current Project] Characterizing the water availability and nutrient transport of Hawaii’s watersheds and its implication to downstream agriculture and coastal environment

current project hydrology water quality

PI: Dr. Yinphan Tsang

This project proposes to characterize the water availability and quality of watersheds in Hawaii. This project proposes to support and train students and research assistants to survey the water quality and quantity and to perform hydrologic analyses. The objectives of this study include:

  1. Research and document the current status of water availability and nutrient loading in watersheds of Hawaii.
  2. Describe the nutrient loading, transport, and nutrient dynamic in Hawaii’s watersheds.

This project proposes to gather preliminary data on water availability and quality, and ultimately aim to apply to large federal agency proposals. The potential future study topics include but not limit to: a) Assessing the hydrological and ecological impact of stream diversions in Hawaii streams, b) the cropland, and costal ecosystem, and c) the rainfall-runoff response of urban dominated watershed in Hawaii. The goal of this project is to establish a baseline of water quantity and quality of watersheds. Ultimately, we would like to better inform water resources management to agriculture, urban area, and coastal environment.

Current status:

  • Establish the current nutrient loading condition for watershed Hawaii
  • Conduct additional survey or study to describe the dynamics of water availablity and nutrient process at selected watershed
